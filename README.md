## 📌 Project Overview

This project is a **multi-page HR Employee Analytics Dashboard** built in **Power BI**, covering **200 employees** across four departments — Engineering, Sales, HR, and Marketing — over the period **2021 to 2025**.

The dashboard enables HR teams and leadership to explore workforce metrics interactively, with filters for **Education Field**, **Department**, and a **Year range slider (2021–2025)**. It surfaces critical patterns around headcount distribution, compensation equity, training ROI, and attrition risk in a single, self-service report.

---

## 🎯 Project Objective

- Identify departments and job roles with the highest attrition risk and support data-driven retention planning.
- Evaluate whether training investment translates into proportional salary growth.
- Analyse compensation distribution across departments and gender to detect pay inequities.
- Understand how overtime workload correlates with employee burnout and attrition trends.
- Track salary hike trajectories over five years to assess pay competitiveness.
- Deliver a filterable, insight-forward tool that empowers HR decision-making without relying on static reports.

## 💡 Project Insights

1. **Overtime is a systemic risk** — 71.5% of the workforce is on overtime, far above sustainable levels. This is the most likely primary driver of the 17.5% attrition rate, particularly in Engineering.

2. **Training ROI is declining** — Sales Executives receive the most training hours in the organisation, yet income per training hour has been falling since 2022. Training is not being rewarded through compensation or promotions.

3. **Engineering and Marketing have the highest attrition rates** — Despite Engineering being the largest department, its attrition rate is the highest, making replacement costs disproportionately heavy.

4. **Young talent aged 25–35 is the most at-risk group** — This cohort shows the sharpest attrition peak, likely driven by limited career mobility, high overtime, and stagnating salary growth.

5. **Non-binary employees are significantly undercompensated** — With only 5.8% of total income share, this group requires an urgent pay equity review and structured progression support.

6. **Salary hike momentum has stalled** — After peaking in 2023, total salary hike percentages declined through 2024 and 2025. In a high-overtime environment, this is a textbook condition for sustained elevated attrition.

---

## 📝 Final Conclusion

The 17.5% attrition rate is not a single-cause problem. It is the compounding result of **excessive overtime, declining salary competitiveness, and training investment that goes unrewarded** — hitting hardest in Engineering and among employees aged 25–35.

**Priority actions to reverse the trend:**
- Cap and monitor overtime in Engineering, the largest and most at-risk department.
- Restructure Sales Executive training programmes to tie completion to measurable income outcomes.
- Restore salary hike momentum, especially in the post-2023 period where the decline is sharpest.
- Conduct a formal pay equity audit for non-binary employees and address the 5.8% income share gap.

This dashboard is designed to enable precise, targeted interventions — every visual is filterable by Department, Education Field, and Year range, ensuring HR teams act on facts rather than assumptions.
